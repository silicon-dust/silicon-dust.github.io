    Exec

    The Exec is an off-the-record operative serving the interests of their hypercorp. Take the exec if you want to take an active part in the direction the group is going, want to hold power over others and are ok with enacting potentially ruthless directives.

    Exec moves

    Enacting directives

    As an executive for a hypercorp, you are contractually obligated to enact the directives of the Board, your superiors in the corpo-hierarchy.

    • When you succeed at enacting a directive, remove 2 from the Board Clock.
    • When you fail to enact a directive, add 1 to the Board Clock.
    • When you attract too much heat, add 1 to the Board Clock.
    • When you ask for additional budget, add 1 to the Board Clock and gain 1 Budget.
    • When you succeed at securing a valuable asset or position by your own initiative, remove 1 from the Board Clock.
    • When you fail to secure a valuable asset or position due to your lack of initiative, add 1 to the Board Clock.

    The MC may give, change, pause and cancel directives at any moment during play as they deem appropriate. The board owes you no justification.

    Budget and bonuses

    When the Board gives you a new directive, you gain 1 Budget to enact this directive.

    You may not ask for a favor with your corporation. Instead, you may spend a Budget to acquire a Favor from them.

    Additionally you may spend 1 Budget at any time to get :

    • A 10-cred cryptowallet to pay your employees, buy gear and services.
    • Reshelling facilities and emergency funds providing a +1 Lifestyle to another player when their Resurrection insurance activates, cancellable at any moment.
    • Temporary access to a special, rare or expensive technology or service. Go wild.

    If you should remove a segment to your board clock due to Enacting directives and have no segments filled, gain a 5-cred bonus instead per segment you should have removed this way. This bonus is a reward for services rendered and the Board will not expect it to be used to further the objectives of the corporation.

    Privileges

    As an executive for a hypercorp, you live in luxury. Your corporation provides you with Lifestyle 3 regardless of the contents of your stash. Be careful: Your Privileges are the first thing you lose if your corporation cuts you off.

    Big fish

    When you Intimidate someone by mentioning who you work for and what kind of enemies they are about to make, you may use +Memetics instead of +Kinesics. Additionally, you may add 1 to the Board clock to treat a miss as a partial or a partial as a success. If you do, tell us what your corporation is doing behind the scenes to sway your target.

    Blackmail +Memetics

    When you use your hypercorp secret databases to get leverage on someone, roll +Memetics.

    Success

    You say what you have on them.

    Partial

    The MC says what you have on them.

    Miss

    They're clean, at least on the record.

    Creative accounting

    When you want to buy something for yourself or another player and you ask your corporation for help , it costs -1-cred. 1-cred items are free but the M.C will tell you how many you can get your hands on.

    Transhuman ressources

    Once per session, when you need the services of an expert or a squad, your hypercorp gives it for free. You may get a Tier II expert or squad. Pay +1-cred for a Tier III and +5-cred for a Tier IV.

    VIP +Memetics

    When you want to arrange a meeting with someone, send them your details and roll +Memetics.

    Success

    They'll be there.

    Partial

    They agree to the meeting but they may have taken additional precautions.

    Miss

    They'll be there, but it could be a trap.

    Character creation

    Starting moves

    You start with the Enacting directives, Budget and bonuses and Privileges moves.

    Corporation

    Your corporation is a Tier 4 faction. Remind the MC then answer the following:

    What are your corporation products (choose 1 main and 1 or 2 secondary):

    • AI, robots and drones.
    • Armament and military services.
    • Biotechnology.
    • Communication systems, surveillance and memetics.
    • Cryptobanking services.
    • Cybersecurity and intelligence.
    • Entertainment and information.
    • Habitat construction.
    • Manufacturing and heavy industry.
    • Mining, refining and energy.
    • Shells and augmentations.
    • Transportation services and logistics.

    How is their corpo-culture (choose 1 or 2):

    • Based on an ancient or new religion.
    • Money is Honor, Debt is Trust.
    • Pragmatic and ruthless.
    • Secrets, Codes, Initiations and Masters.
    • They think they are gods.
    • Vicious and perverted.

    Finally, name your corporation.

    The Board

    You start with an unfilled 6-segment clock representing your Board. The Board is the entity you answer to in the corporate structure. They give you your resources, your status and in exchange, you enact their directives. As a grey-ops agent you have almost no oversight but you are expected to produce results. The Board Clock has 6 segments that can be filled or cleared, it represents the attitude the board has towards you. The less the Board Clock is filled, the more they're satisfied with your work. The more it's filled, the more impatient they are becoming. During play, segments are filled or cleared by the Enacting directives move.

    When you fill the sixth and last segment of the Board Clock, you're fired. Lose all your Exec moves instantly. The board is likely assigning a special unit or another exec to your termination.

    You now have two choices:

    • Change to another archetype and be hunted by your old employers. Create the archetype as normal but keep your old expertise. Ignore gear, background and link creation rules, picking a new name may prove handy however.
    • Try to impress your Board by giving them an extremely valuable asset in exchange for your old job. Reset your board clock to 3.

    Expertise

    Choose 1 expertise and follow its creation rules to the end, this will give you your expertise moves, your shell, your starting credits and your gear.

    Background

    Choose 1 background from the list, answer its questions.

    In addition, answer these questions too:

    • What part of you does the corporation own?
    • If you escaped, what would your risk if they caught you?
    • Was there ever a directive you refused to enact?
    • Is there a rival corporation? What are they like?

    Links

    Ask 2 questions to other players and let them answer if they want to:

    • Who here is an operative I employ ? Give them 1 cred
    • Who here has been one of my informants in the past ? Give them 1 cred
    • Who here owes my corp, and why? MC gives them 3 creds, they owe your corp 10, but you could negotiate something with your board...
    • Who here refuses to have any dealings with me ? Why ? Give them nothing

    Factions

    Choose a faction with which you start with +1 and another with which you start with -1. Explain why. You can't choose the faction you're working for.

    Name

    Finally choose a first name and/or a last name.

    First name: Anton, Asa, Ashr, Cadence, Celyn, Edard, Haral, Hayama, Julia, Kaelyn, Lena, Liu, Maev, Maxin, Morgan, Naeem, Oshin, Shua, Trev, Tsvetka, Violet, Xav, ...

    Last name: Alvarez, Brock, Hewett, Kane, Larsen, Lowell, Malenko, McPherson, Mitsu, Nu-Xia, Ovakov, Schroeder, Voight, Ying, Yukori, Zephyr, ...

    XP and Improvements

    When you roll a highlighted stat, you gain 1 XP.

    When you spend 5 XP, you gain a new move from your archetype or your expertise(s). If you already had at least 5 improvements, you may also spend 5 XP to gain a new expertise. You only gain access to its moves this way, no starting gear.

    Lifestyle and Stash

    You start with a personal Lifestyle 0 and an empty Stash. But your Privileges gives you a Lifestyle 3 while you're an Exec.

    • Lifestyle 0 : no food, no energy, no place.
    • Lifestyle 1 / Stash : 5-cred : enough resources to live and a basic place to stay.
    • Lifestyle 2 / Stash : 15-cred : luxe resources and a nice place.
    • Lifestyle 3 / Stash : 30-cred : the hyper-elite lifestyle.

    You may increase your lifestyle by storing cred in your stash.
