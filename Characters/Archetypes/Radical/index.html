    Radical

    The Radical is an activist that fights for its cause. Play the Radical if you want to be the leader of an ideological faction and if you want to push forth your cause whatever the cost.

    Radical moves

    Social change and Dissent

    Something is fundamentally wrong in this habitat and you're here to bring social change. At the first session, define what kind of social change you want to bring and ask the MC how this habitat is ripe for change: between 0 and 3 segments are prefilled on your Social change clock.
    Being a leader isn't easy, dissent grows in your ranks as you push your agenda and break your promises. Your Dissent clock represents the ramping divide in your faction and it begins empty.

    Fill 1 segment of your Social clock:

    • When you severely hurt your enemy's interests.
    • When you show the world the damage done by your enemy.
    • When a respected figure takes your side.

    Remove 1 segment of your Social clock:

    • When your faction's interests are severely hurt.
    • When damage done by yourself or your faction becomes public.
    • When a respected figure takes side against you.

    Fill 1 segment of your Dissent clock:

    • When your faction's interests are severely hurt.
    • When you break a pledge.
    • When you do something contrary to your faction's values.
    • When you associate with enemies.

    Remove 1 segment of your Dissent clock:

    • When you give your faction what they want.
    • When you remove internal dissension.

    When the Social change clock is full, you bring social change in this habitat : laws are passed, history is written, power is transferred, people riots, mentalities evolve, ... As a result, increase your faction's Tier by 1. In addition, if it makes sense, another faction may decrease by 1 Tier. Social change is not always a clean process, your Cause wins, but there may be costs to be paid or casualties: ask the MC. Finally, reset your Social change clock and define what's your next step.

    When the Dissent clock is full, your faction breaks up or is brutally attacked, your authority crumbles or you're betrayed. Decrease your faction's Tier by 1 and reset the Dissent clock.

    Favors

    You may not use the Favors move with your faction. Instead, each session, you may acquire as many free assets as your faction tier. You may still ask for Favors as usual with other factions.

    Benefits

    Leading a faction comes with benefits. Choose as many as your faction's Tier :

    • You have +1 when your Resurrection insurance activates.
    • You have a permanent squad of your Tier.
    • You have a permanent expert of your Tier.
    • You have access to +4-augs.

    Each time your faction increases or decreases in Tier, add or remove 1 more accordingly.

    Preach +Memetics

    When you preach the Cause to a neutral or friendly crowd, roll +Memetics.

    Success

    Hold 3.

    Partial

    Hold 1.

    Spend your hold 1 for 1 :

    • A NPC becomes friendly or loyal to your Cause.
    • The crowd does something collectively (protest, celebrate, share, testify, give up to an emotion).
    • You can ask a question from Read a person about the crowd or someone in it.
    • You don't make enemies.
    • The crowd goes back to their business.

    Sworn enemy

    When you directly act against an enemy of the Cause or to further your Cause, you have +1d-ongoing.

    Utopia

    Your faction claims control of an habitat. If your faction's Tier is lower than the habitat's Tier, you have a base inside the habitat. If it's equal or higher, you take control of the habitat, you're the new Overton Window here. You may remove previous Habitats moves and effects. Describe your utopia.

    Character creation

    When you create your character at the first session, mark your starting moves, choose a Cause, generate your Faction, choose an expertise, choose a background, ask your link questions and finally choose a name. Follow the guide below.

    Starting moves

    You start with the Social Change and Dissent and Favors moves.

    The Cause

    Choose your Cause :

    • Anti-hypercapitalism : Hypercops should be destroyed.
    • Anarchism : No gods, no masters.
    • Anarcho-capitalism / Minarchism : Welfare and authoritarian states should be abolished.
    • Artificial sentience rights : Artificial sentiences and lifeforms should be recognized as people.
    • A new religion : You're a zealot for a new religion, which one ?
    • An old religion from earth : You're a zealot for an old religion, which one ?
    • Anti-specism : All organisms should have equal rights.
    • ______________ .

    What does your Cause entails ? What makes of you an extremist even for this Cause ? How does your Cause judges society ? How do you differentiate between allies and enemies to the Cause ?

    The Faction

    Choose a faction in this habitat that are your allies. You get +1 Rep with them.

    Choose a faction in this habitat that are your ennemies. You get -1 Rep with them.

    Choose a faction in this habitat from which you are dependent. Why are you dependent on this faction ?

    Describe what kind of assets (transhuman ressources, gear, turf, ...) your faction has.

    Answer as many as you want :

    • How is your vision of the Cause more radical than the average interpretation ?
    • Where do your Faction's member meet ?
    • How is your Faction organised ?
    • What is your Faction's symbol or brand ?
    • What are the subgroups in your Faction ? How do they differentiate ?
    • Who is the average member of your Faction ?

    Expertise

    Choose 1 expertise and follow its creation rules to the end, this will give you your expertise moves, your shell, your starting credits and your gear.

    Background

    Answer yourself (Pick 2):

    • What was the moment you awakened to your Cause ?
    • Who or what did you left behind for your Cause ?
    • You had a mentor, who were they ?
    • How were you oppressed for your Cause ?

    Links

    Ask the table (Pick 2):

    • Who here is open to my Cause ? Why ?
    • Who here is prejudiced against my Cause ? Why ?
    • Who here have I employed to further my Cause ? What was it for ?

    XP and Improvements

    When you roll a highlighted stat, you gain 1 XP.

    When you spend 5 XP, you gain a new move from your archetype or your expertise(s). If you already had at least 5 improvements, you may also spend 5 XP to gain a new expertise. You only gain access to its moves this way, no starting gear.

    Lifestyle and Stash

    You start with a Lifestyle 1.

    • Lifestyle 0 : no food, no energy, no place.
    • Lifestyle 1 / Stash : 5-cred : enough resources to live and a basic place to stay.
    • Lifestyle 2 / Stash : 15-cred : luxe resources and a nice place.
    • Lifestyle 3 / Stash : 30-cred : the hyper-elite lifestyle.

    You may increase your lifestyle by storing cred in your stash.
