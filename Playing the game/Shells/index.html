    Shells

    With the digital mind comes the realization that our bodies are mere shells, replaceable, a commodity. Bodies used to be extensively augmented but now we change them at will, we reshell. We design them conform to our cultural identities, our preferences. Shells are mirrors of our paradigms.

    Shells are one of the core concepts of characters. They represent your bodies and their abilities. Shell are composed of several components:

    • Stats, Kinesics, Memetics, Process and Sensors, which are the most common kind of bonuses for moves.
    • Augs, which represents the various augmentations of this particular shell.
    • A Harm Clock, to track how much harm this shell has suffered.
    • A type and a look, which allows generation of aesthetic flavor for your shell.
    • A downside, every shell has at least one.

    Stats

    There are 4 main stats in Silicon Dust :

    • Kinesics means the precision, speed and strength of your shell.
    • Memetics means the social utilities embedded in your shell.
    • Process means the pure computational power of your MPU.
    • Sensors means the quality and diversity of your shell's sensory capabilities.

    These 4 stats may go from +0 to +4 where +0 is the base human level. By default they're at 0 but you may buy stats as augs to increase their associated bonus. See Making moves for more details on how to use these stats.

    Augs

    Augs, short for augmentations, are technological improvements from base human.

    • Augs can be acquired by spending cred during ellipsis.
    • Augs can be broken. (for stat augs, it means -1).

    Harm Clock

    A shell is a physical object susceptible to destruction and as such your shell tracks the amount of harm it suffered. When a shell's Harm Clock is full, this shell is forever destroyed and you automatically make the Resurrection insurance move.

    See Harm for more details.

    Type and look

    There are 3 types of shells :

    • Bioshells : biological shells, most are fast grown in vats, but some still from an uterus (or a cloaca, or an egg...). While the humanoid form is endlessly declined, uplifted and chimera templates also exist on the market.
    • Synthshells : synthetic shells, made from metal, plastic, silicon, usually robot looking. The design varies wildly but and almost all are marked by the stigma of lower quality as the sensory experience of synthshells usually don't have the "organic" quality of their biologic counterparts.
    • Infoshells : not really a shell, more like a custom server somewhere and you present as a digital avatar. You exist only on the Grid but you may integrate in augmented reality in most habitats.

    In your shell handout, your type determines a list of options to determine your look.

    Downside

    Finally, all shells have a downside. Your shell's downside can be a lot of things and functions as fictional positioning : it states something true in the fiction. At character creation, players choose their character's shell downside. When changing shells, whether because you buy a new one or your resurrection insurance activates.

    This list, also found in the Resurrection insurance move, may help you to find an interesting downside : tracked, previous owner, corporate override, unresponsive, buggy perception, addiction, wild controls, psycho-affective trouble, chronic pain, ...

    Resurrection insurance & Reshell

    Your shell may die. When that happens, your Resurrection insurance kicks in and you have to make hard choices on your new shell. When it's done, you may Reshell, meaning reuploading your mind in this new shell.
