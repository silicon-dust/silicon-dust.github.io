    Harm & Trauma

    Trauma

    The digital mind freed us from corporeal death. Our physical integrity matters less and less as we reshell when we die. But while physical harm has easy fixes, our traumas, on the contrary, stay with us.

    Trauma represents last longing psychological harm and differs from traditional harm in several ways.

    First, it can't be easily healed. Second, you keep it when you reshell and is tracked in your archetype instead of your shell.

    PC Trauma

    When a character suffers trauma, fill 1 segment of your trauma clock on your archetype handout. Then make the Suffer trauma move with a base dice pool equal to your total trauma (the number of segments filled in your trauma clock).

    NPC Trauma

    When a NPC suffers trauma, the MC decides what happens to them. They may loose their shit all together or it may change their personality in subtle ways. They may go in a blind rage or run away never to be seen again. In any case, when a NPC suffers trauma, the MC is free to go hard on them.

    Harm

    Harm represents the physical damage inflicted to characters. As a tag, harm is noted n-harm.

    PC Harm

    Harm clock

    Shells have a harm clock which represent its physical integrity. At character creation, the harm clock have 6 unfilled tracks.

    Suffering harm

    As a PC, when you suffer harm :
    1. Substract your n-armor to the n-harm suffered. If the harm has the ap tag (armor penetration), ignore this step.
    2. Roll the Suffer harm move with the harm suffered (minus armor if applied) as your dice pool and establish consequences according to the move.
    3. Fill as many tracks of your Harm Clock as the harm suffered.

    If your harm clock is at 4 or more, you're in a critical state. Your state will worsen unless you get appropriate help.
    If your harm clock goes to 6, your shell dies. What happens next depends on the situation and your resurrection insurance.

    Healing/Repairing harm

    By default, it costs 1-cred to remove the first 3 segments of your harm clock. For each additional segment past the first 3, it cost +1-cred.

    Repairing a broken aug costs 1-cred for each aug repaired.

    NPC Harm

    When NPC suffers harm, the same rules for armor and ap apply but NPC don't make moves and the consequence of harm is determined by this scale :

    • 0-harm : Concussions.
    • 1-harm : Blood.
    • 2-harm : May be fatal if left unchecked.
    • 3-harm : Likely fatal.
    • 4-harm : Fatal and gore.
    • 5-harm and more : Bodily destructive.

    Things Harm

    For small objects, if you may have what it takes to destroy them, you destroy them, no problem. Most things will be destroyed with 1-harm or 2-harm this way.

    For vehicles and buildings, consider that only harm with the breach tag will add up while normal harm doesn't stack up.

    To destroy bigger things like entire arcologies and habitats, you will need special measures and arrangements.
