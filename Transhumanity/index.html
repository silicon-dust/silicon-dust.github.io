    Ego technology

    Immortality

    Due to the invention of mind digitization technology, transhumans are now immortals. Their immortality resides in the fact that their neural topology can be finely monitored and stored as data, allowing them to be resurrected in case their body dies. If dead, this brain snapshot can be put in a new body or run in a virtual environment on a computer. Almost everyone has, as a basic augmentation, a cortical stack that acts as this internal backup, constantly monitoring and updating its data to contain to the latest events experienced. Because of this technology, new terminology came around, the digitized extended mind was called the ego and the replaceable bodies were called shells.
    In addition, it is not unusual to have backups in cold storage at home or in resurrection insurance mindbanks in case your cortical stack has been destroyed. There are all manner of policies regarding these past backups but usually after some time, the most recent backup is resurrected and is considered the person that died. Usually this happens rarely though, only when people have their cortical stack destroyed and not connected to the Grid.
    Death now refers to the destruction of the shell and permadeath has come to refer to the permanent destruction of all backups of an ego.

    Forks

    The logical conclusion of mind digitization was copying oneself by putting a backup in additional shells. This is known as forking and transhumans created this way are known as forks. The limit between a fork and a resurrected individual is a complex legal and metaphysical debate and changes between polities. Usually, forking is illegal because of the demographic explosion risk associated to it. There are many exceptions to this rule but it is often done in the shadows. Forks and the original are often forced to merge or to suicide themselves until only one remains.

    Mechanically, when a player character forks themselves, they make the Fork move. In that case, they may control and play their multiple forks. While a player character has forks, they must occasionally make the Diverge move to see if they loose narrative control of one of their forks.

    Pseudoforks

    Pseudoforks are simple AI updated with basic models of your beliefs, motivations and personnality. They come in various levels of refinements and are used to send representatives, whether because of a busy schedule or to overcome speed of light latency between distant habitats. Advanced pseudoforks are usually illegal as there have been cases of pseudoforks going rogue. They are usually hunted done in that case.

    Psychosurgery

    With the ego digitized, it was possible to edit it as data, an art known as psychosurgery. Minds are now upgraded with artificial networks integrated using psychosurgery. Psychosurgery also allows to add and remove memories, beliefs, emotions. Transhumanity is able to intentionally edit its own mind. Some only edit out minor bad memories but some have edited so much as they are barely recognizable as human minds. Advanced and experimental psychosurgery may lead to trauma and psychiatric diseases like paranoïa, delusions, personality changes, depression, ... It also allowed to create new kinds of minds called neuromorphs.

    Mechanically, for player characters, acts of psychosurgery may be performed by having the Psychosurgery expertise or acquiring the services of an expert with these skills.

    Neuromorphs

    Base: Only minor modifications.
    Construct: Not so much a specific kind of neuromorph, but serves to identify fully artificial minds, whatever their inner architecture. They are usually simply called AI and rarely admitted to legal personhood in most habitats.
    Immutables: They disabled neural plasticity in their value centers and deleted most or all pathways related to emotions, they also sometimes deleted all their memories from before. They're known to be cold, devoted to a cause and incorruptible. Sometimes becoming an immutable is required by polities and corporations for some roles. They often are argumentative and exhaustive in conversations.
    Precogs: They wove many feedback pathways between unrelated parts of their brain. They are known to be hypersensitive, intuitive and unreliable. They are creative and improvisational. They often speak in strange metaphors and aphorisms.
    Uplift: They were animals before being uplifted to sentience. They added metacognition, planning and higher order knowledge to their animal brain. They still have desires and behaviors of their previous mindless animals. Uplifiting is rare and highly regulated in most polities.
    Seraphims: They're a rare and experimental agglomerate of multiple forks of the same individual. Illegal everywhere except in some outer habitats.
    Crowdmind: Similar to seraphims but the agglomerate contains different people that fused. They experience every point of view at the same time allowing them to form more abstract thoughts about reality. They talk in generality and are often dedicated to a communal cause. They're illegal almost everywhere except in their own habitats or in highly unregulated habitats.

    Reproduction

    Because of immortality, the laws quickly changed in order to avoid a demographic explosion. Permits to reproduce must be issued by most polities to have children in order to control the demographics of habitats. Unlicensed children often requires parents to be forcefully reshelled in infoshells to pay for the ressources

    The Grid

    The Grid is the transhuman evolution of internet. From internet it kept the concept of being a communication network. But the Grid is much more than that. It's an ecosystem of various subnetworks, communications systems, protocols and habitat and things meshes all connected to one another through gateways and backdoors. The Grid varies from habitat to habitat. Habitat subnetworks come in all shades of surveillance, transparency, encryption level, anonymity, accessibility, services, reputation systems, ... In habitat with fascistic tendencies it may be hard to connect to the rest of the Grid, you may need a contact with access to a stealth satellite or admin access. In others, the Grid may easily be accessed but is easily traceable if you do not take proper precautions. In free habitats, anonymity may be a protocol-level feature allowing for free and anonymous access to the Grid.

    Grid inserts

    Everyone, or almost everyone, has grid inserts in their shell, which are a set of augmentations designed to wirelessly connect to the Grid and interface it with your sensory systems. Grid inserts lets you and your angel access information on the grid, add layers of holograms in your perception and enter simulspaces.

    Accessing the Grid

    Some systems offer free and secured access to the Grid, but others have ego verification measures and automatic surveillance AIs. In general, even if there is an authoritarian regime in charge, there is always some resistance running micro-satellites providing a connection to the rest of the Grid, you just have to find them.     Alternate reality and the Mesh

    The Grid is also composed of layers and layers of virtual elements covering the physical reality of habitats. Your perception of habitats is enriched of holographic displays, spatialized sounds and even virtual taste and flavor. Parts of this alternate reality comes from your applications : a map application will display a minimap in your view field or indicate markers in the environment, social utils and network clients may print a data bubbles and public profiles of people surrounding you, weapon detection systems may highlight detected weapon in your view field, ...
    Another layer of alternate reality comes from your direct environment. Most habitat structures and objects are directly interconnected forming the Mesh : the aggregate of embedded data and utilities. Doors may give you access data, the lightning system of rooms may be accessible, holographic advertising and tags cover the walls, weapons may print information such as the remaining ammo and the coils temperature, ...
    Off-grid, society looks weird, people talk to invisible people, they make movements in the air, they seem to see things that are not there, ...

    When you create an habitat, ask yourself what is the basic experience of the local mesh ? Is it standardized or diverse ? Minimalistic or saturated ? Functional or aesthetic ?

    When you create your character, ask yourself how you configure your alternate reality settings ? What utilities you always have directly available ? What kind of experiences your angel filters or lets through in your perception ?

    Virtual reality and simulspaces

    Another aspect of the everyday experience of transhumanity is virtual reality. Simulspaces are artificial virtual spaces allowing seemingly real experiences by transmitting perceptual data directly into grid inserts. Simulspaces of various sizes, populations and designs coexist in the Grid. Some are huge immersive games, others are virtual offices for corporations, others are artificial landscapes for virtual tourism, others are individual relaxation spaces, others are huge social cities, ... What you can do in simulspace depends on its programming. Some sandbox simulspaces give a lot of possibilities to build things but others only lets you move. The physics of simulspaces may be very realistic or outright weird with non-euclidean physics or anything in between. Simulspace time may go faster than real time but there may be limits to that.
    All shells allow to create their own personal simulspaces and grid inserts allow people to access simulspaces on the Grid. While you may be blocked doing things in simulspaces, you may always log out as long as you have control of your grid inserts. However, an cortical stack running in an egoloader may not be able to log out allowing for effective accelerated torture.

    Society

    Human societies evolved into transhuman societies. ego technology removed the fear of death and profoundly shifted the spiritual values. Old religions couldn't keep up with the leap and new religions emerged. After the Fall, old states perished leaving the corporations taking the Overton window. They associated under the name of the Consortium.

    Economy

    AI automation and the progress of science allowed for production chains capable of producing most goods for cheap and in mass. However, except on earth where everything is available, other habitats on the system depends on costly imports on the ressources they lack locally. On Venus, air is cheap but water is expensive for instance. This ripples locally on individual habitat economies.

    So everyone still has to work.
